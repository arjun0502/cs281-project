One simple technique is called the “5-4-3-2-1” grounding exercise. It helps you reconnect with the present moment by using your senses. Here’s how it works:

1. **Five things you can see**: Look around you and notice five things. They could be colors, objects, or anything that catches your eye.
2. **Four things you can touch**: Identify four things you can physically feel. This could be the texture of an item, the feeling of your clothes against your skin, or even the air on your face.
3. **Three things you can hear**: Listen closely and identify three sounds. It could be the hum of an appliance, birds chirping, or even your own breathing.
4. **Two things you can smell**: This might be a little tricky if you’re not in a fragrant space, but try to notice two scents. If you can't identify something now, think of your two favorite smells.
5. **One thing you can taste**: This could be the taste of something you just ate or a sip of a drink.
